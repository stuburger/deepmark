DeepMark works with schools to mark student work. Although DeepMark staff do not have direct, unsupervised contact with children, the service processes work produced by children — including, on occasion, free-text answers in subjects such as English, RE, PSHE, and History where students may write about distressing or sensitive topics. We take our responsibilities seriously and have adopted this policy to make clear how we handle safeguarding concerns.

This policy is informed by **Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSiE)** and **Working Together to Safeguard Children**. It does not replace the safeguarding policy of any school using the service — schools remain responsible for the welfare of their pupils.

## 1. Our commitment

DeepMark is committed to:

- The welfare of every child whose work is processed through our service
- Treating each child's work with confidentiality and respect
- Acting promptly and appropriately on any disclosure of harm
- Working in partnership with schools' Designated Safeguarding Leads (DSLs)
- Ensuring everyone working at DeepMark understands their safeguarding responsibilities

### 2.2 The school

The school using DeepMark remains the lead organisation for safeguarding decisions about its pupils. The school's DSL is responsible for receiving disclosures, making referrals to children's social care or the police, and following the school's own safeguarding procedures. DeepMark will support the school but does not replace it.

### 2.3 Everyone at DeepMark

Every member of DeepMark staff and every contractor with access to student work is required to:

- Read this policy and acknowledge it as part of induction
- Complete safeguarding awareness training appropriate to their role
- Know how to recognise a possible safeguarding concern in student work
- Know how and when to escalate to the DSL

## 3. Recognising a safeguarding concern

A safeguarding concern can arise in many ways. Examples that may appear in marked work include:

- A student describing physical, sexual, or emotional abuse — at home, at school, or elsewhere
- References to self-harm or suicidal ideation
- Indications of neglect (hunger, lack of care, lack of supervision)
- References to exploitation, including criminal exploitation, sexual exploitation, or "county lines"
- Reference to forced marriage, female genital mutilation, or so-called honour-based abuse
- Indicators of online harm, including grooming or coercion
- Disclosures of being a young carer in unsafe circumstances
- Disclosures of radicalisation or extremism (Prevent duty)

Care should be taken not to over-interpret creative writing, and not to under-react when something genuine is being communicated. When in doubt, escalate.

## 4. What we do when a concern is raised

If a member of DeepMark staff identifies a possible safeguarding concern in student work, or such a concern is reported to us:

1. **The same working day**, the staff member must inform the DeepMark DSL.
2. The DSL records the concern, including what was seen or heard, the date, and the source.
3. The DSL contacts the relevant school's DSL **without delay**, providing enough information for the school to act, and following any school-specified reporting protocol.
4. Where a child appears to be at imminent risk and the school cannot be reached, the DSL will contact the relevant local authority children's social care team or the police directly, in line with KCSiE.
5. DeepMark records the action taken and follows up with the school as appropriate.
6. Information is shared on a need-to-know basis only and stored securely. Confidentiality is respected so far as is consistent with the child's safety.

## 5. AI processing and safeguarding

DeepMark uses AI to extract text and suggest marks. AI processing is **decision support, not assessment**. AI cannot reliably identify safeguarding concerns and we do not use it for that purpose. Nothing in this policy implies that AI processing replaces a human teacher's judgement about a child's wellbeing.

If a teacher reviewing AI-suggested marks notices content that raises a concern, they should follow their school's safeguarding procedure in the normal way. The DeepMark interface is designed to surface the original student work clearly so that a teacher can see what was written.

## 6. Online safety

DeepMark is a closed platform: only authenticated teachers and school administrators with an account may access scripts. Students do not log in to DeepMark. Sharing of student work outside the school account is restricted to functions provided by the platform (PDF download, sharing within the school) and is logged.

Schools using DeepMark are responsible for ensuring that staff with accounts:

- Use strong, unique passwords and enable any available multi-factor authentication
- Do not share account credentials
- Lock or sign out of devices when leaving them unattended
- Treat student work as confidential

## 7. Recruitment and training

DeepMark applies safer recruitment principles to roles with access to student data, including:

- Identity verification
- Right-to-work checks
- References for staff in roles with elevated access
- Acceptance of this policy and the Privacy Policy as a condition of access

All staff complete safeguarding awareness training on induction and at least annually thereafter. The DSL completes Level 3 (or equivalent) safeguarding training and refreshes this every two years.

## 8. Allegations against staff

If an allegation is made that a member of DeepMark staff has behaved in a way that has harmed, or may have harmed, a child, the matter will be reported to the Local Authority Designated Officer (LADO) for the area in which the staff member lives or works, in line with KCSiE Part 4. The staff member will be subject to internal investigation alongside any external process.

Reports of staff conduct concerns should be sent to [safeguarding@getdeepmark.com](mailto:safeguarding@getdeepmark.com), or to the company directors at the registered office address, where the DSL is the subject of the concern.

## 9. Working with schools

Schools using DeepMark are encouraged to:

- Tell us about any specific protocols we should follow when reporting concerns about their pupils
- Raise any concerns about how DeepMark has handled student data or a safeguarding matter

We are happy to provide a Data Processing Agreement and to complete safeguarding due diligence questionnaires.
